Remembering Martin Luther King Jr. and his role in the Civil Rights Movement

Timeline: Major events in the life of Martin Luther King Jr.

  • Jan/15/1929 - Michael King born in Atlanta. His father would later change oth of teir names to Martin in1934.
  • Sept/20/1944 - King enrolls at Morehouse College at age 15.
  • 6/Aug/1946 - A letter about the rights of minorities by King is published by The Atlanta Constitution.
  • 25/Feb/1948 - King is ordained and becomes assistant Pastor at Ebenezer Baptist Church
  • 18/June/1953 - King and Coretta Scott are married
  • 1/Sept/1954 - King begins his pastorate at Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery,Alabama.
  • 1/Dec/1955 - Rosa Parks is arrested for refusing to vacate her seat on a Montgomery City for a white passenger.
  • 5/Dec/1955 - King become president of the newly formed Montgomery Improvement Association,leading the Montgomery bus boycott.
  • 13/Nov/1956 - The U.S. Supreme Court affirms the decision in Browderv. Gayle, declaring Alabama's bus segregation laws unconstitutional and ending the 381 day protest in Montgomery.
  • 10/Jan/1957 - King is named of what becomes the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
  • 10/May/1957 - King gives his first national address,"Give Us the Ballot" at the Lincoln Memorial at the Prayer Pilgrimage for Freedom.
  • 23/June/1958 - King and other Civile rights leaders meet with President Dwight D.Eisenhower in Washington.
  • 3/Feb/1959 - King visits India, meeting Prime Minister Jawaharial Nehru and many of the late Gandhi's followers.
  • 16/Apr/1959 - King pens his "Letter from Birmingham Jail" while in Jail for ignoring an Alabama state court injunction against demonstrations.
  • 28/Aug/1963 - King delivers his famous "I Have a Dream" speech at the Lincoln Memorial to over 200,000 demonstrators at the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom.
  • 3/Jan/1964 - King is named Time's "Man of the Year".
  • 26/March/1964 - King meets Malcom X, fro the first and only time, in Washington.
  • 10/Dec/1964 - King receives the Nobel Peace Price at 35, he is the youngest recipient so far.
  • 10/Dec/1964 - King receives the Nobel Peace Price at 35, he is the youngest recipient so far.
  • 17-25/March/1965 - King, James Forman, and John Lewis lead civil rights marchers from Selma to Montgomery after voting rights marchers are beaten in Selma on "Bloody Sunday."
  • 4/Apr/1967 - King delivers a speech, "Beyond Vietnam," declaring his opposition to the war, and demanding the U.S. take new initiatives to ending the conflict.
  • 4/Dec/1967 - King reveals for a mass civil desobedience campaign to push the government to end poverty.
  • 28/March/1967 - King leads six thousand protestors in support of striking sanitation workers in Memphis. The march ends in looting and violence
  • 3/Apr/1968 - King returns to lead a peaceful protest, and delivers in his final speech, "I've Been to the Mountaintop."
  • 4/Apr/1968 - King is shot and killed on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel in Memphis.
